Accelerating the Renewable Energy Transition in the Philippines.

Understanding the path to a cleaner, more resilient future through renewable energy solutions in the Philippines.

The Philippines is rapidly advancing its renewable energy infrastructure, driven by ambitious national goals and institutional reforms aimed at reducing reliance on fossil fuels. Under the Philippine Energy Plan 2023–2050 and the National Renewable Energy Program, the government has committed to increasing the share of renewable energy from around 22 percent in 2022 to 35 percent by 2030 and 50 percent by 2040

A major milestone was achieved in June 2025, when the Department of Energy launched its first offshore wind‑only Green Energy Auction (GEA‑5), offering 3,300 megawatts of fixed‑bottom offshore wind capacity, with expected deliveries between 2028 and 2030. Onshore projects are also expanding. The Balaoi and Caunayan Wind Farm in Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte is set to become the country's largest onshore wind farm, with 160 MW capacity generated by 32 turbines supplied by Siemens Gamesa

Solar development is equally vigorous. Construction began in 2024 on the Meralco Terra Solar Farm, a massive 3,500 MW solar‑plus‑battery project spanning Bulacan and Nueva Ecija, expected to be fully operational by 2026

In Ilagan City, Isabela Province, a P18‑billion, 440 MW solar PV project is underway and will begin feeding electricity into the grid in 2025. Renewables are already delivering measurable cost savings. In the first half of 2025, spot electricity prices in the Philippines dropped to 4.14 pesos/kWh, down from 5.58 pesos/kWh in 2024. The Independent Electricity Market Operator projects that prices could fall by up to 24 percent by 2029 thanks to increased renewable output and greater use of natural gas.

To support this energy shift, the Department of Energy has also tightened regulations on stalled projects and aims to reallocate unused contracts to active developers The World Bank has approved financing that is expected to raise the share of renewables in installed capacity from 30 percent in 2023 to 42 percent by 2027, funding offshore wind capacity and energy efficiency initiatives.
