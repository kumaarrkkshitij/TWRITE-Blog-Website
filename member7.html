The Importance of Sleep

Understand the harmful effects of sleep deprivation and why getting enough sleep is essential for overall health.

Sleep is one of the most important parts of our daily routine, yet it’s often neglected. In today’s fast paced world, work is usually given the utmost importance and hence prioritized too. Many people sacrifice rest for work, social media, or other form of entertainment. However, getting enough quality sleep is essential for both physical and mental health.

The ideal sleep duration required for adults is between the range of 7 to 9 hours, depending on individual preference. Unfortunately, modern lifestyles, especially those involving late night screen use, have contributed to the disruption of this routine. More than the number of hours, the quality of sleep also matters. Even if someone sleeps for a long time, poor sleep quality can still result in fatigue and reduced focus the next day.

Millennials are known for their long working hours, but for Gen Z, staying up late has become more of a trend. In fact, one article revealed that over 93% of Gen Z admitted to staying up late just because of social media. While it might seem harmless, this habit can actually affect sleep quality. The blue light from screens can seriously affect the quality of rest. That’s why experts recommend turning off electronic devices at least 2 to 3 hours before bed to get better sleep.

Lack of sleep has been linked to serious health issues, including heart disease, dementia, cancer, diabetes, and chronic insomnia. It has been stated by Dr. Arvind Bhateja, a neurosurgeon in India, that sleep deprivation can be more dangerous than alcohol consumption. During sleep, the body gets to fully recover and recharge.

In addition to limiting screen time, people are also advised not to eat heavy meals before bedtime. Foods high in fat or sugar like fast foods such as pizza, can affect the sleep quality. Eating high calorie meals or heavy food before sleeping tends to make the body focus on digestion, which reduces the quality of sleep. To ensure good quality, it’s best to avoid heavy meals at least 2 to 3 hours before going to bed, so digestion is complete by bedtime.

Thanks to evolving technology, there are now several apps and wearable devices that help track sleep patterns. People who struggle with sleep, like those dealing with sleep disorders or frequent disruptions, should monitor their habits and try to reduce the activities that might be leading to this. If things don’t improve, it’s always a good idea to talk to a doctor. Sleep issues are real, and ignoring them can make things worse over time.

In conclusion, sleep should not be treated as something optional but as a daily necessity. It affects overall health and how we function. By understanding its importance and making it a priority, we can improve our quality of life. After all, health is wealth !
